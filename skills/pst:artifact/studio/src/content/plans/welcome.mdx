<Section title="How you'd ship it" aside="Prescriptive">
  <Steps>
    <Step title="Author the artifact" priority="P0" effort="S" owner="claude">
      `/pst:artifact` writes a bespoke MDX page into the studio and opens `astro dev`.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Review with click-anywhere comments" priority="P1" effort="S" owner="you">
      Drop pins anywhere; each captures the nearest anchor, section, and local
      text. Threads save to `feedback/<id>.json`.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Revise from feedback" priority="P1" effort="M" owner="claude">
      `/pst:artifact --feedback <id>` reads the threads back and edits the page.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Publish" priority="P2" effort="S" owner="you">
      `/pst:artifact --publish <id>` builds, syncs to S3, invalidates CloudFront, and
      prints the shareable URL.
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</Section>

<Section>
  <Callout tone="tip" title="Authoring rule of thumb">
    Lead with the outcome, vary the rhythm, and put a `data-anchor` on every
    meaningful unit so it's commentable. If two adjacent sections use the same
    block, you're drifting back toward a wall of text — switch the composition.
    See <Link href="https://docs.astro.build/en/guides/content-collections/">Astro content collections</Link> for how pages are wired.
