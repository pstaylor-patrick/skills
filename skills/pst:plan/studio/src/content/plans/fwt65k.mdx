<Section title="What shipped" aside="The capability" tint>
  <Features cols={3}>
    <Feature icon="sparkle" title="Bespoke, not boilerplate">
      Each plan composes a kit of blocks and picks its own palette and type, so
      two artifacts never look like the same template.
    </Feature>
    <Feature icon="map" title="Click-anywhere comments">
      Drop a pin anywhere; it captures the nearest section, element, and
      surrounding text, then feeds the revision back to Claude.
    </Feature>
    <Feature icon="link" title="Amazon-style URLs">
      <code>/p/&lt;id&gt;/&lt;slug&gt;</code> — the short id is canonical, the slug
      is cosmetic and can change. Typos still resolve.
    </Feature>
    <Feature icon="shield" title="Private by default">
      No-index / no-follow at the edge. These are working documents, not public
      pages.
    </Feature>
    <Feature icon="gear" title="Bring your own infra">
      One Terraform apply provisions S3 + CloudFront + ACM + Route53 in your own
      AWS account. Convention over configuration.
    </Feature>
    <Feature icon="chart" title="Production feel">
      An optional, off-by-default per-artifact view counter — the polish that
      says "shipped," not "tossed up."
    </Feature>
  </Features>
</Section>

<Section title="The review loop" aside="In practice" tint>
  <Steps>
    <Step title="Author" priority="P0" effort="S" owner="claude">
      `/pst:plan` writes a bespoke page and opens it in the browser.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Comment" priority="P1" effort="S" owner="you">
      Click anywhere to leave grounded feedback; it saves to disk automatically.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Revise" priority="P1" effort="M" owner="claude">
      `--feedback <id>` reads the comments back and edits the artifact.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Publish" priority="P2" effort="S" owner="you">
      `--publish <id>` ships it to your subdomain and prints the link.
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</Section>
